The Three Mentions of Demas

The arc is the whole point.

Colossians 4:14
"Luke the beloved physician, and Demas,
greet you."

The co-laborer. Present. Trusted. Named.

1 of 3
Philemon 1:24
"Marcus, Aristarchus, Demas, Lucas,
my fellow labourers."

Still in the inner circle. Still working.

2 of 3
2 Timothy 4:10
"For Demas hath forsaken me, having loved
this present world."

Paul doesn't say Demas became wicked. He says Demas loved something else more.

The Core Tension

Good Work vs. First Love

Both truths are real. The tension is the point.

Hold both of these at the same time

God calls us to meaningful work in the world.

The world can quietly replace God in our hearts.

The tension between them is not a contradiction. It is the permanent condition of the Christian life.

Ellen White  ·  The Great Controversy, p. 508
"Satan works to distort the character of God — often making Him seem harsh or unloving — to distract humans, breaking their focus and preventing a close, personal relationship with Him."
— Ellen G. White

Biblical Reorientation

Christ at the Center

Matthew 6:21
"For where your treasure is,
there will your heart be also."

The heart follows what you value. The treasure reveals what you love.

Revelation 2:4 — To the Church at Ephesus
"Nevertheless I have somewhat against thee,
because thou hast left thy first love."

A church that worked harder than any other. But the heart had moved.

Ellen White  ·  The Acts of the Apostles, p. 547
"The church of Ephesus had let slip her first love. She was more intent on carrying on the forms and ceremonies of religion than on possessing its spirit. She was more anxious to win the approval of men than to win the approval of God."
— Ellen G. White
"The Sabbath is God's built-in resistance against becoming Demas."
The Sabbath as Corrective

It interrupts. It recenters.

Not a rule. A rescue.
It interrupts
  • Identity through achievement
  • Constant striving
  • Replacement of God by good things
It recenters
  • Relationship over performance
  • Trust over productivity
  • Devotion over deliverables
"Demas still looked connected to ministry,
but his heart had drifted from Jesus.

He lost his first love before he lost his way."
